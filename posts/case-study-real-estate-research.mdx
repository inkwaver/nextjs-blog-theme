### Listing Card structure
#### Introduction to Minimal Necessary  Fields for Listing Cards

{' '}

<br />
Listing cards are a key element on real estate websites, providing a snapshot of
property information to users. During our research, we encountered a variety of card
configurations, each with different levels of complexity. Given the repetitive nature
of listing cards, it is crucial to include only the most necessary information to
maintain clarity and usability. After consulting with realtors, we have determined
that our listing cards should focus on displaying the property image, essential details
such as the number of bedrooms and bathrooms, price, and location. Additionally,
the card should include an actionable button and serve as a link to the property’s
detailed page. This streamlined approach ensures that users receive the information
they need at a glance without being overwhelmed by excessive details.
<br />
### Key Points:
<div className="col-50">
  - Concise Information: Listing cards should present essential property details
  in a clear and concise manner, making it easy for users to quickly assess and
  compare properties. - User Interest: By focusing on the most relevant
  information, listing cards can effectively capture and maintain user interest.
  - Realtor Input: Consultations with realtors have guided us to include only
  the property image, basic details (number of bedrooms and bathrooms, price,
  and location), and an actionable button. - Navigation: Each listing card will
  link directly to the property's detailed page, providing a seamless user
  experience and easy access to more comprehensive information.
</div>
<br />

### Conclusion:

   <div className='col-50'>
    In summary, the design of our listing cards will prioritize simplicity and functionality, ensuring that users have access to all the critical information they need without unnecessary clutter. This approach not only improves the user experience but also aligns with best practices in real estate web design.
